What We Handle

EB-5 Investor Visas. H&H Law has processed 2,000+ EB-5 petitions for investors seeking permanent residence through qualifying U.S. investments. See our EB-5 Investor Visa page.

Employment-Based Immigration. EB-1, EB-2 (including National Interest Waiver), EB-3, and PERM labor certification for professionals and the Orange County employers who hire them. See Employment-Based Immigration.

Family Immigration. Petitions for spouses, parents, children, and siblings — immediate-relative and preference categories, with realistic timelines from the Visa Bulletin. See Family Immigration.

Green Cards & Adjustment of Status. Form I-485 adjustment for applicants already in the U.S., and consular processing for those abroad — including guidance under the 2026 USCIS discretionary-review policy. See Green Card & Adjustment of Status and our analysis of the 2026 USCIS adjustment of status policy change.

Naturalization. Form N-400 citizenship applications, eligibility review, and interview preparation. See Naturalization.

Serving Irvine

Irvine is home to many of the professionals, researchers, and international families we work with — from UCI scholars and tech-sector employees on H-1B and O-1 visas to business owners pursuing EB-5. While our office is in Santa Ana (about 15 minutes from central Irvine via I-5 or SR-55), most matters begin with a consultation that can be held in person or by video. For employment-based cases, we work directly with Irvine employers and HR teams on PERM, I-140, and I-485 strategy.

Why an Immigration-Only Firm

Immigration law changes quickly — USCIS policy memoranda, Visa Bulletin movement, and shifting adjudication standards can change a case's strategy mid-stream. Because H&H Law practices immigration law exclusively, our attorneys track these changes as a matter of daily practice. Recent example: our published analysis of USCIS Policy Memorandum PM-602-0199 (May 2026) and what it means for pending I-485 applicants.
